Discussion

Key Findings & Methodology

The analysis reveals that the conventional narrative of California patent dominance is a product of population size as much as innovation intensity.

Coastal Concentration

CA, NY, WA, MA, and OR account for the majority of raw output — reflecting concentration of tech, pharma, and biotech industries in coastal metro areas.

Per Capita Surprises

DC (343.6 per 100K) leads by a substantial margin, followed by WA (85.6), MA (100.8), and VT (69.5). Small, innovation-dense geographies rise dramatically when normalised.

Midwest Manufacturing

Michigan (55.9), Minnesota (80.9), and Wisconsin (33.5) show strong per-capita performance driven by automotive, medical device, and industrial manufacturing innovation.

Boston's Strength

The Boston-Cambridge metro (5,949 grants) ranks 5th nationally. Massachusetts ranks top-3 per capita, anchored by biotech and pharmaceutical R&D in the Route 128 corridor.

Silicon Valley Gap

San Jose metro produced 14,618 patents — 1.5× San Francisco (9,732) and over 2× any non-California metro. The concentration of semiconductor and software IP is unmatched nationally.

Data Pipeline

USPTO CBSA bulk files parsed with custom Python logic. State extracted from MSA name string. Aggregated via pandas. Merged onto Census TIGER/Line boundaries with GeoPandas. Visualised with Matplotlib (static) and Plotly (interactive).
